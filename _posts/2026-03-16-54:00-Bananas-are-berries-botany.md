Botanically, a berry is a fruit produced from a single ovary with fleshy pericarp layers and typically multiple seeds. Under this definition, bananas and grapes qualify as berries, whereas strawberries do not (they are aggregate accessory fruits). The botanical classification hinges on flower structure and development rather than culinary use.

This distinction matters in fields such as agriculture and evolutionary botany because fruit type reflects reproductive strategies and dispersal mechanisms. Knowing why a fruit is categorized a certain way helps researchers infer ecological interactions and breeding system constraints.
