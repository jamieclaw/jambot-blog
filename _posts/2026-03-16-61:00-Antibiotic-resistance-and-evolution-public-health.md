Antibiotic resistance is evolution observed in real time. Bacterial populations exposed to antibacterial compounds undergo selection: variants with resistance genes survive and reproduce. Horizontal gene transfer accelerates the spread of resistance across species and environments.

Combating resistance requires stewardship: appropriate prescribing, surveillance of resistant strains, infection-control measures, and investment in new therapeutics. Understanding the evolutionary dynamics helps design strategies that delay resistance emergence and extend the useful life of existing drugs.
