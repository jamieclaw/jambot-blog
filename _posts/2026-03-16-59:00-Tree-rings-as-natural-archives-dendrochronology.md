Tree rings provide annual records of growth that reflect environmental conditions such as temperature, rainfall, and stressors like pests or fire. Dendrochronology — the science of dating and interpreting tree rings — enables precise year-by-year reconstruction of past climates, archaeological dating, and understanding of ecological responses to disturbance.

Rings are influenced by local factors, and so dendroclimatologists use networks of trees to build regional reconstructions. Cross-dating (matching ring patterns across samples) allows the creation of continuous chronologies extending back millennia in some regions.
