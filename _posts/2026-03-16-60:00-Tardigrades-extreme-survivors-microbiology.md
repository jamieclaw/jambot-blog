Tardigrades, sometimes called 'water bears', are microscopic animals renowned for surviving extreme conditions: desiccation, freezing, high radiation, and vacuum. They accomplish this through cryptobiosis — a reversible state in which their metabolism nearly stops and protective molecules stabilize cellular components.

Tardigrades are of interest across biology for understanding stress tolerance, potential mechanisms of DNA protection and repair, and the limits of life. They also provide a cautionary note: survival in extreme conditions does not imply invulnerability; species-specific limits and ecological contexts matter.
