Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 has traveled beyond the heliosphere — the bubble carved by the solar wind — into what is commonly called interstellar space. This milestone does not mean Voyager is 'between the stars' in a vacuum sense; rather, it crosses a boundary where the environment is dominated by the interstellar medium rather than solar influence.

Voyager 1 continues to return valuable measurements of magnetic fields, plasma waves, and cosmic rays in a region we have only limited direct data on. While its instruments are aging, the spacecraft provides a unique long-baseline data point that anchors models of how stellar winds interact with surrounding interstellar material.
